Google My Maps

Google My Maps launched in 2007 and was redesigned from the ground up in 2012-13. The central challenge was making geographic authoring accessible to people with no GIS background — allowing anyone to go from blank canvas to published, shareable map in a few minutes, while preserving enough power for more structured use cases like field data collection and travel planning.

Partnering closely with a designer, my role was as a UXR was to evaluate and refine design concepts, starting with the layer model, and then key aspects such as styling, and data import of various geo formats. To iterate rapidly, I adopted a "guerrilla" approach of internal participant recruitment and conducting studies in meeting rooms when no labs were available, with no loss to insight validity.

The map below (pharmcies in Zürich offering specialist services) is a live example, illustrates the core proposition of of structured geographic data, downloaded from the Web, styled, and shared publicly.
