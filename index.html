About

With a background in natural sciences as a Zoologist at Christ Church, Oxford, I switched to HCI with a Master's degree from the University of London (MSc, distinction), and arrived at the commercial web just as it was getting started, joining Netscape in 1997 as a Usability Engineer. Since then I have led many successful UX Research projects across a number of companies, WebTV, Yahoo!, Zynga, Oracle, and eleven years at Google, split across Geo and YouTube, and most recently a senior research role at Roche.

I am most effective when I am closest to the material, thinking through design problems with code and prototypes rather than documents and decks.

I pioneered a technique of embedding interactive protoypes into survey infrastructure to conduct at-scale behavioural studies, zeroing in on specific aspects of design via the evaluation of micro-tasks. This method bridges the gap between in-person usability studies and live experiments on fully engineered builds, and it still has no commercial equivalent today.

I hold UK, US, and EU citizenship and live in Zürich, Switzerland.
